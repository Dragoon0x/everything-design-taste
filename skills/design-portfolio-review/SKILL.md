# Design Portfolio Review

## What to Look For

### Must Have
- **Problem definition:** Can they articulate the problem?
- **Process evidence:** Did they show how they got to the solution?
- **Decision reasoning:** Can they explain WHY, not just WHAT?
- **Impact:** What happened after they shipped?

### Nice to Have
- Visual craft and polish
- Research and data references
- Before/after comparisons
- Honest reflection on what worked and didn't

### Red Flags
- No problem definition (jumps straight to visuals)
- No mention of users, data, or outcomes
- All projects look the same (one style applied everywhere)
- Can't distinguish their work from team work
- Defensive about feedback in interview

## Portfolio Structure Advice
1. 3-4 case studies (quality over quantity)
2. Most impressive work first
3. Each case study: Problem → Process → Solution → Impact → Reflection
4. 1,500-2,500 words per case study
5. Show the messy middle, not just the polished end
6. Include one project that failed and what was learned

## Interview Questions
- 'Walk me through your decision on [specific element]'
- 'What would you do differently?'
- 'What constraints did you face?'
- 'How did you handle [disagreement/feedback]?'
- 'Show me something you're proud of that nobody notices'
