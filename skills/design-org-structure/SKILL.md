# Design Org Structure

## Team Models

### Centralized
All designers report to a design lead. They're assigned to product teams but their career and craft development lives in the design org.

**Pros:** Consistent quality, strong design culture, clear career paths
**Cons:** Potential disconnect from product teams, resource allocation friction
**Best for:** Companies where design consistency matters more than speed

### Embedded
Each designer reports directly to a product team lead. No central design org.

**Pros:** Fast, tight product alignment, designers have full context
**Cons:** Quality drift, isolation, no design mentorship, career path unclear
**Best for:** Early-stage startups where speed is everything

### Hybrid (most common at scale)
Designers are embedded in product teams but have a dotted line to a central design leader. Regular design org rituals (critique, design system work) keep quality aligned.

**Pros:** Speed of embedded + quality of centralized
**Cons:** Complex management, potential conflicting priorities
**Best for:** Growth-stage companies (50-500 employees)

## Designer-to-Engineer Ratio

### Benchmarks
- Early startup: 1 designer per 8-10 engineers
- Growth stage: 1 designer per 5-7 engineers
- Mature product: 1 designer per 3-5 engineers
- Design-led products: 1 designer per 2-3 engineers

### When to Hire
- Designers are consistently the bottleneck
- Quality is dropping because designers are spread too thin
- You're entering a new market/product area
- Design debt is accumulating faster than you can address it

## Roles and Levels

### IC Track
1. **Junior Designer** — Executes defined tasks, learning the craft
2. **Designer** — Owns features end-to-end, works independently
3. **Senior Designer** — Drives strategy for product areas, mentors juniors
4. **Staff Designer** — Cross-product impact, defines systems and standards
5. **Principal Designer** — Company-wide influence, sets design direction

### Management Track
1. **Design Lead** — Player-coach, manages 3-5 designers
2. **Design Manager** — Full-time people management, 5-8 reports
3. **Director of Design** — Org strategy, cross-functional leadership
4. **VP of Design** — Executive team, company strategy, org building
5. **CDO/Head of Design** — Board-level, brand and product vision

## Design Culture Essentials
- Weekly design critique (skill-based, not approval-based)
- Shared design system with active governance
- Design principles documented and referenced in decisions
- Regular user research exposure for all designers
- Portfolio reviews for career development
- Design all-hands with show-and-tell
